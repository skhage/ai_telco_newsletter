**Key Industry Trends (Week of Nov 4, 2025):**

- **AI Networking Emerges as a Strategic Focus:** Both **Verizon** and **AT&T** are positioning AI-driven networking as a primary growth engine, aiming to monetize next-generation network automation and intelligence^[Artificial Intelligence][6](https://www.fierce-network.com/broadband)[7](https://www.fierce-network.com/broadband).
- **Fiber Expansion and FWA^[Fixed Wireless Access]:** Major carriers are accelerating fiber deployments and leveraging FWA to capture new broadband customers, with **AT&T** acquiring Lumen’s fiber assets and **T-Mobile** and **Verizon** pushing FWA as a bridge to fiber^[Fiber to the Premises][1](https://www.zacks.com/stock/news/2776475/vz-vs-chtr-which-connectivity-stock-is-the-better-buy-now)[2](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/vz-vs-chtr-which-connectivity-stock-better-buy-now)[3](https://techblog.comsoc.org/category/broadband-access-subscriber-growth/).
- **Rural and Underserved Market Initiatives:** **Charter** and **AT&T** are investing heavily to expand high-speed internet to rural and underserved areas, often in partnership with government programs^[1](https://www.zacks.com/stock/news/2776475/vz-vs-chtr-which-connectivity-stock-is-the-better-buy-now)[2](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/vz-vs-chtr-which-connectivity-stock-better-buy-now).
- **Competitive Pricing and Margin Pressure:** The battle between cable and wireless providers is driving down home internet prices, intensifying competition and compressing margins, especially for cable operators^[4](https://www.benton.org/headlines/new-street-research-end-acp-continued-drag-cable-broadband-market).

### AT&T

- **Fiber Expansion:** AT&T is aggressively expanding its fiber footprint, focusing on densifying its existing infrastructure and acquiring Lumen’s fiber business to bolster its network reach^[1](https://www.zacks.com/stock/news/2776475/vz-vs-chtr-which-connectivity-stock-is-the-better-buy-now)[2](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/vz-vs-chtr-which-connectivity-stock-better-buy-now).
- **AI Networking:** The company is betting on AI-powered networking as a future revenue driver, aiming to automate and optimize network operations for both enterprise and consumer markets^[6](https://www.fierce-network.com)[7](https://www.fierce-network.com/broadband).
- **FWA as On-Ramp to Fiber:** AT&T continues to use FWA to attract new broadband customers, with a stated long-term goal of converting these users to FTTH^[Fiber to the Home] as fiber build-outs progress^[3](https://techblog.comsoc.org/category/broadband-access-subscriber-growth/).
- **Legal Dispute:** AT&T filed suit against an advertising watchdog over claims related to its ads targeting T-Mobile, highlighting ongoing marketing battles in the sector^[6](https://www.fierce-network.com).

### T-Mobile

- **Wireless Growth:** T-Mobile posted strong wireless customer growth, with domestic wireless customer lines up 20% year-over-year and net additions of 322,000 in the most recent quarter^[3](https://techblog.comsoc.org/category/broadband-access-subscriber-growth/).
- **FWA and Fiber Strategy:** The company continues its “fiber optic network acquisition binge” to complement its FWA business, aiming to provide high-speed broadband alternatives to traditional cable^[3](https://techblog.comsoc.org/category/broadband-access-subscriber-growth/).
- **Financial Performance:** T-Mobile reported a 7% increase in adjusted EPS and record-high adjusted EBITDA margin of 41.9%, reflecting operational efficiency and network expansion success^[3](https://techblog.comsoc.org/category/broadband-access-subscriber-growth/).
- **Leadership Changes:** The company is navigating executive transitions, a trend also seen at Verizon and Comcast, which may impact strategic direction^[5](https://sundaybrief.com/a-trio-of-ceo-changes/).
