# Data Technology Trends: Week of January 20-23, 2026

## Key Industry Trends

The U.S. telecommunications sector is experiencing a **fiber-driven consolidation wave** coupled with aggressive **5G and satellite connectivity expansion**[4]. Major carriers are prioritizing **converged service models** that bundle broadband, mobile, and enterprise solutions while managing rising fiber build costs in 2026[5]. Additionally, **M&A activity remains robust**, with tier-1 telcos strategically acquiring fiber assets to accelerate network convergence strategies[4].

## Company News

### AT&T

AT&T is preparing for **Q4 2025 earnings release on January 28**, with investors closely monitoring fiber net additions and satellite connectivity progress[1].

**Network Infrastructure Achievements:**
- Deployed **mid-band spectrum from EchoStar across approximately 23,000 cell sites**, delivering significant speed and capacity improvements to customers in 5,300 cities across 48 states[1][2]
- Achieved **80% download speed improvement** for mobility services and **55% improvement** for AT&T Internet Air users following EchoStar spectrum integration[2]
- Activated its **fourth satellite ground gateway** and successfully launched **Bluebird 6**, the largest commercial communications array deployed in low earth orbit, in collaboration with AST SpaceMobile[1]

**Fiber Expansion:**
- Recorded **288,000 net fiber additions** in Q3 2025, with Internet Air adding 270,000 subscribers during the same quarter[2]
- Projects reaching approximately **50 million customer locations** with in-region fiber by end of 2030, plus more than 60 million locations when including Lumen Mass Markets fiber assets[2]
- Introduced **AT&T Guarantee**, the industry's first customer guarantee for both wireless and fiber networks, offering proactive bill credits for service downtime[2]

**Strategic Partnerships:**
- Collaborating with **Mitsubishi Motors North America** to integrate advanced 5G connectivity into the upcoming Mitsubishi Outlander SUV[2]
- Expanding into **public safety and healthcare** verticals to diversify revenue streams beyond legacy telecom[1]

**Market Position:**
- Gained **5.2% stock performance over the past year**, outperforming Verizon (0.8% gain) and T-Mobile (16.5% decline)[1]
- Trading at **10.35x forward P/E ratio**, below industry average of 11.11x and its historical mean of 12.56x[1]

**Headwinds:**
- Facing pressure from declining Business Wireline segment due to customer migration toward IP-based offerings[1]
- Expected lower fiber net additions in Q4 due to seasonal holiday effects[1]
- High debt obligations and rising satellite connectivity infrastructure costs pressuring near-term margins[1]
