**Key Industry Trends (Week of August 25–September 1, 2025)**

- **Major fiber asset consolidation:** AT&T announced a landmark acquisition of Lumen’s Quantum Fiber business, intensifying the race for FTTH^[Fiber to the Home] dominance and sparking speculation about spectrum assets left on the table for rivals like T-Mobile^[T-Mobile US, Inc.] and Starlink^[SpaceX’s satellite internet service][1](https://www.aol.com/t-buying-95-lumen-quantum-235000498.html).
- **Aggressive fiber expansion:** Charter^[Charter Communications, Inc.] and Verizon^[Verizon Communications Inc.] are doubling down on fiber network investments, aiming for multigigabit, symmetrical broadband at scale[4](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/verizon-gains-65-six-months-should-you-invest-vz-stock).
- **Wireless–broadband convergence:** The “big five” (AT&T, T-Mobile, Verizon, Charter, Comcast) now control nearly all U.S. broadband and cellular growth, with cross-selling and bundling strategies accelerating[3](https://potsandpansbyccg.com/tag/t-mobile/).
- **Competitive and regulatory pressures:** Margin compression, regulatory scrutiny, and integration risks loom as companies pursue scale and operational efficiency[5](https://www.ainvest.com/news/4-7-dividend-safe-bet-income-investors-2025-2508/).

## AT&T

- **Acquisition of Lumen’s Quantum Fiber:** AT&T is acquiring 95% of Lumen’s Quantum Fiber business for $5.75 billion, gaining 1 million fiber customers and 4 million fiber locations across 11 states[1](https://www.aol.com/t-buying-95-lumen-quantum-235000498.html)[4](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/verizon-gains-65-six-months-should-you-invest-vz-stock).
- **Fiber expansion strategy:** The company aims to pass 50 million locations with fiber by 2030, leveraging $3.5 billion in tax savings from the One Big Beautiful Bill Act[5](https://www.ainvest.com/news/4-7-dividend-safe-bet-income-investors-2025-2508/).
- **Bundling momentum:** 40% of AT&T’s fiber customers now also buy cellular, reflecting strong convergence[3](https://potsandpansbyccg.com/tag/t-mobile/).
- **Dividend and financials:** AT&T’s 4.7% dividend yield remains attractive but is under scrutiny due to a high payout ratio and regulatory risks, including an ongoing investigation into legacy lead-sheathed cabling[5](https://www.ainvest.com/news/4-7-dividend-safe-bet-income-investors-2025-2508/).

## T-Mobile

- **Spectrum interest:** T-Mobile is reportedly eyeing spectrum assets not included in AT&T’s Lumen deal, potentially to bolster its 5G^[Fifth Generation wireless] and fixed wireless access offerings[1](https://www.aol.com/t-buying-95-lumen-quantum-235000498.html).
- **Customer growth:** T-Mobile added 1.7 million postpaid net customers in Q2 2025, maintaining a low churn rate of 0.90%[5](https://www.ainvest.com/news/4-7-dividend-safe-bet-income-investors-2025-2508/).
- **Market expansion:** The company is set to absorb USCellular’s customer base, further consolidating its position among the “big five”[3](https://potsandpansbyccg.com/tag/t-mobile/).

## Verizon
