**Key Industry Trends in U.S. Data Technology (Week of October 15, 2025)**

- **Fixed Wireless Access (FWA)^[Fixed Wireless Access] and Fiber Expansion:** Major telcos are doubling down on FWA and fiber, with Verizon acquiring Starry to accelerate urban FWA and T-Mobile pushing fiber deeper into new markets[1](https://tecknexus.com/fwa/)[4](https://bbcmag.com/broadband-communities-releases-the-2025-top-100-list/).
- **AI-Driven Network Upgrades:** Lumen is investing heavily in backbone upgrades to support surging AI traffic, with a focus on 400Gbps transport and expanded intercity fiber[1](https://tecknexus.com/fwa/).
- **Leadership and Strategy Shifts:** T-Mobile is transitioning to a new CEO with a sharpened focus on digital transformation and AI, while maintaining operational continuity[1](https://tecknexus.com/fwa/).
- **M&A and Market Consolidation:** T-Mobile and Verizon are both active in acquisitions to boost their broadband and FWA footprints, reflecting a broader industry trend toward consolidation[1](https://tecknexus.com/fwa/)[3](https://www.fierce-network.com/broadband/fierce-networks-2025-telecom-and-tech-ma-tracker).
- **Cable Sector Resilience Amid Policy Shifts:** The end of the ACP^[Affordable Connectivity Program] continues to weigh on cable broadband growth, but cable remains a major economic driver[1](https://tecknexus.com/fwa/)[6](https://www.lightreading.com/broadband/end-of-acp-a-continued-drag-on-cable-broadband-market-report).

### T-Mobile

- **Leadership Transition:** Srini Gopalan will become CEO, succeeding Mike Sievert, who moves to Vice Chairman. The transition is designed to maintain continuity while accelerating digital and AI initiatives[1](https://tecknexus.com/fwa/).
- **Fiber Ambitions:** T-Mobile surpassed 500,000 U.S. fiber households and aims for 12–15 million by 2030, with the pending Metronet acquisition set to boost this goal[4](https://bbcmag.com/broadband-communities-releases-the-2025-top-100-list/).
- **M&A Activity:** T-Mobile acquired U.S. Internet, a regional ISP, further expanding its fiber broadband reach[3](https://www.fierce-network.com/broadband/fierce-networks-2025-telecom-and-tech-ma-tracker).
- **Satellite Connectivity:** In partnership with SpaceX, T-Mobile launched a cellular Starlink service, now the world’s largest satellite-to-mobile constellation with 650 dedicated satellites[4](https://bbcmag.com/broadband-communities-releases-the-2025-top-100-list/).
- **US Cellular Deal:** T-Mobile completed a $4.3 billion acquisition of US Cellular’s wireless operations and spectrum, adding 4 million customers[4](https://bbcmag.com/broadband-communities-releases-the-2025-top-100-list/).
