**Key Industry Trends (Week of September 5, 2025)**

- **Fiber Expansion and M&A:** The U.S. data technology landscape is defined by aggressive fiber^[Fiber] network expansion, major acquisitions, and a shift toward enterprise and AI-ready services. AT&T, Charter, and Lumen are all making significant moves to expand or refocus their fiber assets, while Verizon continues heavy investment in both fiber and 5G spectrum.
- **Cybersecurity Threats:** A major Chinese-backed cyber-espionage campaign, Salt Typhoon, has targeted leading U.S. telecoms, exposing vulnerabilities in core network infrastructure and prompting urgent federal advisories.
- **Competitive Pressures:** T-Mobile's continued wireless growth and Verizon's MVNO^[Mobile Virtual Network Operator] partnerships with cable operators are intensifying competition, especially in the mid-market and fiber broadband segments.
- **Regulatory and Financial Risks:** AT&T faces regulatory scrutiny over legacy infrastructure and must balance high dividend payouts with the need for operational efficiency and debt reduction.

---

### AT&T

- **Fiber Expansion and Acquisitions:** AT&T remains the dominant U.S. Ethernet provider and is doubling down on fiber broadband, aiming to pass 50 million locations by 2030^[4](https://www.ainvest.com/news/4-7-dividend-safe-bet-income-investors-2025-2508/). The company recently closed its $5.74 billion acquisition of Lumen's Mass Markets fiber business, adding 1 million customers and 4 million locations across 11 states^[1](https://www.lightwaveonline.com/home/article/55313518/att-remains-the-dominant-ethernet-provider)[3](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/verizon-gains-65-six-months-should-you-invest-vz-stock).
- **Financial Performance:** Fiber broadband revenue grew 19% year-over-year in Q1 2025, supporting free cash flow (FCF) and a 4.7% dividend yield. However, the high payout ratio and execution risks around integrating Lumen's assets leave little margin for error^[4](https://www.ainvest.com/news/4-7-dividend-safe-bet-income-investors-2025-2508/).
- **Cybersecurity:** AT&T was among the companies confirmed breached by China's Salt Typhoon hacking group, which targeted sensitive call records and network traffic^[5](https://techcrunch.com/2025/08/27/fbi-says-chinas-salt-typhoon-hacked-at-least-200-us-companies/).
- **Regulatory Risks:** The company is under investigation for legacy lead-sheathed cabling, which could result in fines or reputational harm^[4](https://www.ainvest.com/news/4-7-dividend-safe-bet-income-investors-2025-2508/).

### T-Mobile

- **Wireless Growth:** T-Mobile's "Un-Carrier" strategy continues to disrupt the market, with 1.7 million postpaid net additions in Q2 2025 and a churn rate of just 0.90%—the lowest among major carriers^[4](https://www.ainvest.com/news/4-7-dividend-safe-bet-income-investors-2025-2508/).
- **Competitive Impact:** T-Mobile's momentum is putting pressure on AT&T and Verizon, especially as it continues to attract high-value customers and maintain low churn.

---

### Verizon

- **Network Investments:** Verizon is investing heavily in both fiber and 5G, including significant capital expenditures for the C-Band spectrum (3.7–4.2 GHz) and upgrades to its Intelligent Edge Network^[3](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/verizon-gains-65-six-months-should-you-invest-vz-stock).
- **Competitive Challenges:** The company faces increased competition from AT&T and Charter's fiber expansion, as well as from cable MVNO partnerships that threaten its mid-market share^[4](https://www.ainvest.com/news/4-7-dividend-safe-bet-income-investors-2025-2508/).
- **Financials:** Verizon reported 62,000 Fios Video net losses in Q2 2025, reflecting ongoing cord-cutting trends^[3](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/verizon-gains-65-six-months-should-you-invest-vz-stock).
- **Cybersecurity:** Verizon was also confirmed as a victim of the Salt Typhoon cyber-espionage campaign^[5](https://techcrunch.com/2025/08/27/fbi-says-chinas-salt-typhoon-hacked-at-least-200-us-companies/).
