This week in U.S. data technology, the telecom sector saw major moves in fiber infrastructure, satellite-to-cellular integration, and continued consolidation among leading providers. The drive for **FTTH^[Fiber to the Home] expansion**, satellite partnerships, and M&A^[Mergers and Acquisitions] activity is reshaping the competitive landscape, with AT&T, T-Mobile, Verizon, Lumen, and Charter Communications each making strategic announcements or being impacted by industry shifts.

---

## Key Industry Trends

- **Aggressive fiber expansion**: Major players are racing to acquire and build out FTTH networks, with AT&T and T-Mobile leading recent M&A activity.
- **Satellite-to-cellular integration**: T-Mobile and SpaceX are advancing direct-to-device satellite services, intensifying competition in rural and underserved markets.
- **Market consolidation**: Acquisitions of regional fiber providers and spectrum assets are accelerating, with implications for both consumer choice and network reach.
- **Regulatory and policy shifts**: Ongoing debates over broadband affordability, digital equity, and net neutrality continue to influence provider strategies.

## Company-Specific News

### AT&T

- **Major Fiber Acquisition**: AT&T will acquire most of Lumen’s fiber coverage in 2026, including the CenturyLink and Quantum Fiber networks. This move will significantly expand AT&T’s FTTH footprint, especially in regions where Lumen had a strong presence. The deal is expected to impact millions of customers and further consolidate AT&T’s position as a national fiber leader[4](https://www.allconnect.com/news)[1](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/).
- **Wi-Fi Extender Update**: AT&T is rolling out a gateway software update that will disable two legacy Wi-Fi extender models, requiring customers to upgrade or pay to maintain current coverage[4](https://www.allconnect.com/news).
- **Regulatory Response**: AT&T has withdrawn its home internet service from New York in response to the state’s Affordable Broadband Act, which caps qualifying plans at $15/month[4](https://www.allconnect.com/news).

### T-Mobile

- **Fiber Expansion via M&A**: T-Mobile, through its joint venture with KKR, has acquired U.S. Internet (USI), a Minneapolis-based FTTH provider serving over 140,000 homes and businesses. USI’s residential customers will transition to T-Mobile Fiber, while commercial clients move under Metronet Commercial. This acquisition boosts T-Mobile’s fiber reach to over 2.6 million locations across 19 states, with ambitions to reach 12–15 million by 2030[1](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/)[3](https://bbcmag.com/broadband-communities-releases-the-2025-top-100-list/).
- **US Cellular Acquisition**: T-Mobile completed a $4.3 billion deal to acquire wireless operations and selected spectrum from US Cellular, adding 4 million customers and further strengthening its spectrum holdings[3](https://bbcmag.com/broadband-communities-releases-the-2025-top-100-list/).
- **Satellite-to-Mobile Leadership**: In partnership with SpaceX, T-Mobile has launched a cellular Starlink service, now the world’s largest satellite-to-mobile constellation with 650 dedicated satellites. This enables direct-to-device connectivity, especially in rural areas, and positions T-Mobile as a leader in NTN^[Non-Terrestrial Network] services[3](https://bbcmag.com/broadband-communities-releases-the-2025-top-100-list/).
- **5G Home Internet Upgrades**: T-Mobile introduced faster 5G Home Internet plans and a new Backup Internet option, enhancing its fixed wireless access (FWA) offerings[4](https://www.allconnect.com/news).

- **Divestiture of Fiber Assets**: Lumen is set to transfer most of its fiber coverage—including the CenturyLink and Quantum Fiber brands—to AT&T in 2026.
