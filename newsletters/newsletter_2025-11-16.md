**Key Industry Trends (Week of Nov 10–16, 2025):**

- **Fiber-to-the-Home (FTTH)^[Fiber to the Home] Expansion:** Major U.S. telcos and cable operators are accelerating FTTH deployments, driven by consumer demand and competitive pressure. M&A activity is surging, with Tier 1 players acquiring assets to scale up fiber reach and efficiency[2](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/articles/2025/10/moorgate-sees-fiber-demand-driving-m-a-among-large-midsize-operators-into-2026-94055375).
- **Fixed Wireless Access (FWA) Growth:** FWA continues rapid adoption, especially in urban and underserved areas, as operators leverage spectrum and millimeter-wave^[mmWave] technology to deliver high-speed broadband[1](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/).
- **Consolidation & M&A:** Strategic acquisitions are reshaping the landscape, with notable deals involving AT&T, Verizon, Charter, and T-Mobile, as operators seek scale, operational efficiencies, and expanded fiber footprints[2](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/articles/2025/10/moorgate-sees-fiber-demand-driving-m-a-among-large-midsize-operators-into-2026-94055375).
- **AI & Data Center Infrastructure:** Investor demand for AI-optimized, sustainable data centers is rising, with hyperscale and cloud deployments driving new infrastructure investments[1](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/).

## AT&T

- **Acquisition of Lumen Fiber Assets:** AT&T announced the acquisition of Lumen Technologies’ fiber assets, a move that will significantly boost AT&T’s FTTH footprint and accelerate its transition away from legacy broadband infrastructure[2](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/articles/2025/10/moorgate-sees-fiber-demand-driving-m-a-among-large-midsize-operators-into-2026-94055375).
- **Fiber Expansion:** AT&T remains the largest global fiber network provider, with aggressive plans to add millions of new FTTH connections over the next five years[3](https://telcomagazine.com/top10/top-10-global-fibre-network-providers).
- **AI & Rapid Turnup:** AT&T is leveraging rapid turnup waves and AI-driven network optimization to meet surging demand for data services and cloud connectivity[5](https://www.fierce-network.com/keyword/artificial-intelligence-ai).

## T-Mobile

- **Metronet & USI Acquisition:** Metronet, now jointly owned by T-Mobile and KKR, acquired U.S. Internet (USI), a Minneapolis-based fiber provider. USI’s residential customers will transition to T-Mobile Fiber, expanding T-Mobile’s FTTH reach in Minnesota and supporting its national fiber growth strategy[1](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/).
- **Fixed Wireless Leadership:** T-Mobile continues to drive fixed wireless subscriber growth, now a major force in the FWA market alongside Verizon and AT&T[2](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/articles/2025/10/moorgate-sees-fiber-demand-driving-m-a-among-large-midsize-operators-into-2026-94055375).
- **Text-to-911 Expansion:** T-Mobile expanded text-to-911 availability, enhancing emergency communications for more users[6](https://www.lightreading.com/series/industry-press-releases).

## Verizon

- **Starry Acquisition:** Verizon announced plans to acquire Starry, Inc., a fixed wireless broadband provider operating in major U.S. cities. This deal will integrate Starry’s mmWave technology and urban assets into Verizon’s fiber and spectrum infrastructure, adding ~100,000 subscribers and strengthening Verizon’s FWA portfolio[1](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/).
