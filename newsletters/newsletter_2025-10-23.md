### AT&T

- **Acquisition of Lumen Fiber Assets:** AT&T completed its $5.75B acquisition of Lumen Technologies' mass markets fiber assets, significantly expanding its FTTH footprint and positioning itself for further convergence of wireless and wireline services[1](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/articles/2025/10/moorgate-sees-fiber-demand-driving-m-a-among-large-midsize-operators-into-2026-94055375)[3](https://onetouchintelligence.com/insights/).
- **Fiber Expansion:** AT&T is a major driver behind the projected 40 million new fiber homes over the next five years, with ongoing greenfield and overbuild projects[1](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/articles/2025/10/moorgate-sees-fiber-demand-driving-m-a-among-large-midsize-operators-into-2026-94055375).
- **Fixed Wireless Access (FWA):** AT&T expanded its Internet Air FWA service to 16 new markets, signaling a push to compete with T-Mobile and Verizon in this segment[3](https://onetouchintelligence.com/insights/).
- **Strategic Focus:** CEO John Stankey emphasized convergence (integrating wireless, fiber, and satellite) as a core strategy on the Q3 call[2](https://www.fierce-network.com).

### T-Mobile

- **Fiber Network Ambitions:** T-Mobile is actively pursuing new open-access fiber partnerships, including talks with Tillman FiberCo to become an anchor tenant, signaling a deeper move into the fiber broadband market[3](https://onetouchintelligence.com/insights/).
- **Fixed Wireless Data Cap:** T-Mobile instituted a soft data cap for its 5 million FWA subscribers, introducing data prioritization to manage network congestion as usage grows[3](https://onetouchintelligence.com/insights/).
- **Satellite-to-Cellular Race:** T-Mobile is preparing for a competitive summer in satellite-to-cellular connectivity, aiming to be among the first to offer D2D services[3](https://onetouchintelligence.com/insights/).
- **Spectrum Deal:** T-Mobile and Comcast agreed on a lease for 71 spectrum licenses in the 600 MHz band, covering 149 million people, to bolster T-Mobile's network capacity[3](https://onetouchintelligence.com/insights/).
