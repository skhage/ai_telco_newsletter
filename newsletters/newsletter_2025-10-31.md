**Key Industry Trends (Week of October 31, 2025)**

- **Fiber and 5G Expansion:** Major U.S. telcos are accelerating FTTH^[Fiber to the Home] and 5G rollouts, with AT&T and Charter investing heavily in fiber, while Verizon and T-Mobile push 5G coverage and enterprise solutions[1](https://tecknexus.com/keyword/att/)[2](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/vz-vs-chtr-which-connectivity-stock-better-buy-now).
- **AI and Cybersecurity:** Operators are integrating AI for call screening and launching new cyber defense centers, reflecting a shift toward data-driven, resilient network operations[1](https://tecknexus.com/keyword/att/).
- **M&A and Spectrum Acquisitions:** The industry is seeing billion-dollar deals in fiber, AI, and spectrum, with AT&T’s acquisition of Lumen’s fiber assets and a major spectrum purchase from EchoStar[1](https://tecknexus.com/keyword/att/).
- **Enterprise and Edge Compute:** T-Mobile and Verizon are emphasizing edge compute, cloud, and enterprise services as growth vectors beyond traditional consumer connectivity[1](https://tecknexus.com/keyword/att/)[2](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/vz-vs-chtr-which-connectivity-stock-better-buy-now).

### AT&T

- **Q3 2025 Results:** AT&T reported revenue of $30.7B (up 1.6% YoY), with strong performance in wireless and fiber. Free cash flow rose to $4.9B, supporting debt reduction and capital returns[1](https://tecknexus.com/keyword/att/).
- **Fiber and Mobility Cross-Sell:** AT&T’s strategy to cross-sell fiber and wireless is yielding results in net additions and churn control[1](https://tecknexus.com/keyword/att/).
- **Network Expansion:** AT&T went live on Boldyn Networks’ neutral-host 5G infrastructure in NYC’s Joralemon Street subway tunnel, setting a benchmark for underground connectivity[1](https://tecknexus.com/keyword/att/).
- **AI Call Screening:** Testing a carrier-grade AI “digital receptionist” that screens unknown calls at the network level, aiming to reduce robocalls and improve customer experience[1](https://tecknexus.com/keyword/att/).
- **Major Acquisitions:** AT&T is acquiring Lumen’s fiber assets and has agreed to buy 50 MHz of low- and mid-band spectrum from EchoStar for $23B, covering 400+ U.S. markets. This will densify AT&T’s spectrum holdings and narrow the mid-band gap with competitors[1](https://tecknexus.com/keyword/att/)[2](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/vz-vs-chtr-which-connectivity-stock-better-buy-now).
