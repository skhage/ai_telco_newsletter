### Key Industry Trends in U.S. Data Technology (Week of January 20-26, 2026)

The U.S. telecommunications sector is experiencing a **fiber frenzy** and sustained **M&A boom**, with major players aggressively expanding fiber networks amid rising deployment costs projected for 2026.[3][5][6] Fiber broadband deployments remain in high gear, but a Fiber Broadband Association survey indicates costs will increase again this year due to labor, materials, and supply chain pressures, even as operators like AT&T, Verizon, and Charter push multi-gigabit symmetrical services to millions of locations.[2][6] AI integration is emerging as a tailwind, with collaborations like Charter's work with **AWS^[Amazon Web Services]** for generative AI to boost software development and efficiency.[2] Overall, 2026 outlooks predict steady performance for telcos, with AT&T, Verizon, and T-Mobile expanding fiber footprints by 10-11 million homes, though M&A risks and shareholder returns pose ongoing challenges.[8] Wireless spectrum deals and satellite-to-cell integrations continue to reshape data connectivity, while rural broadband investments accelerate under federal partnerships.[2][3]

### AT&T News and Announcements
AT&T continues to lead in telecom **M&A^[Mergers and Acquisitions]** and fiber expansion, building on its May 2025 $5.75 billion acquisition of **Lumen Technologies^[formerly CenturyLink]**'s consumer fiber business, which bolsters its network to over 60 million fiber locations by 2030 including Lumen assets.[1][2] In Q3 2025, the Communications segment saw wireline revenues rise 4.1% to $3.56 billion, driven by 288,000 net fiber additions and 270,000 **Internet Air** (fixed wireless) subscribers.[2] Recent mid-band spectrum deployment from its August 2025 $23 billion EchoStar deal covers 23,000 cell sites across 5,300 cities in 48 states, boosting mobility download speeds by 80% and Internet Air by 55%.[2] AT&T introduced the industry-first **AT&T Guarantee**, offering bill credits for fiber downtime over 20 minutes or wireless over 60 minutes.[2] Partnerships include Mitsubishi Motors for 5G in the Outlander SUV and **AST SpaceMobile** for satellite connectivity to fill terrestrial gaps.[2] No major new announcements this week, but ongoing integration supports long-term data growth amid competition from Verizon and T-Mobile.[2]

### T-Mobile News and Announcements
T-Mobile announced **SuperMobile** on January 19, 2026, a new service poised to supercharge its business ambitions by enhancing mobile data capabilities, potentially leveraging prior Starlink direct-to-cell integration from July 2025.[4] Building on its August 2025 $1.02 billion **UScellular** acquisition (wireless assets) and spectrum deals with Verizon and AT&T, T-Mobile benefits from UScellular's pivot to a tower company.[3] Q3 2025 saw stock dip 7.67% yearly, but mobile lines grew robustly; a November 2025 CEO appointment of Srini Gopalan signals leadership for data-focused expansion.[1] Concerns linger that AT&T's Lumen fiber deal could challenge T-Mobile's fixed wireless strategy.[4] No additional spectrum or fiber-specific updates this week, but SuperMobile positions T-Mobile for converged 5G and broadband dominance.[4]
