**Key Industry Trends (Early September 2025)**

- **Fiber Expansion and Consolidation:** AT&T^[American Telephone & Telegraph] is accelerating its fiber broadband expansion, acquiring Lumen’s consumer fiber business to reach more U.S. homes and businesses. The U.S. Carrier Ethernet market remains stable but highly competitive, with AT&T maintaining dominance while Lumen, Verizon, and Charter face both opportunities and revenue pressures[1](https://www.lightwaveonline.com/home/article/55313518/att-remains-the-dominant-ethernet-provider)[3](https://www.ainvest.com/news/reaffirms-fiscal-2025-guidance-confirms-capital-return-plans-2509/).
- **Cybersecurity Threats Escalate:** A major Chinese state-backed hacking campaign (“Salt Typhoon”) has targeted all the major telecoms—AT&T, Verizon, T-Mobile^[T-Mobile US], Lumen^[Lumen Technologies], Charter Communications—compromising sensitive call data and prompting urgent security advisories from the FBI[4](https://www.sovereignmagazine.com/science-tech/artificial-intelligence/why-salt-typhoon-hack-changes-everything-about-national/)[5](https://techcrunch.com/2025/08/27/fbi-says-chinas-salt-typhoon-hacked-at-least-200-us-companies/).
- **AI-Centric Network Investments:** Providers like Lumen are pivoting toward AI-ready network architectures to serve enterprise customers’ growing demand for private connectivity fabrics that support artificial intelligence workloads[1](https://www.lightwaveonline.com/home/article/55313518/att-remains-the-dominant-ethernet-provider).

### Company-Specific News

#### **AT&T**
- Remains the leading U.S. Ethernet provider.
  
  - In Q2 2025, *Consumer Wireline* revenues rose 5.8% year-over-year to $3.54 billion due to strong momentum in fiber broadband.
  
  - Net additions: **243,000 new fiber subscribers**; Internet Air added another 203,000.
  
  - Nationwide *fiber footprint* now covers **30 million locations** (consumer + business).
  
  - Announced acquisition of Lumen’s consumer fiber business for $5.75 billion: adds ~1 million customers and expands reach by another ~4 million locations across eleven states.

    > “Allows us to invest and focus on our core enterprise capabilities while also significantly improving our balance sheet.” — Chris Stansbury (Lumen CFO) [3](https://www.ainvest.com/news/reaffirms-fiscal-2025-guidance-confirms-capital-return-plans-2509/)
    
#### **T-Mobile**
  
No significant new data technology announcements this week were reported in available sources; however:

  - T-Mobile was named as one of several major carriers targeted by the Salt Typhoon cyberattack campaign[4](https://www.sovereignmagazine.com/science-tech/artificial-intelligence/why-salt-typhoon-hack-changes-everything-about-national/) alongside peers.

#### **Verizon**

Similar trends as above:

  - Continues as a top-tier Ethernet provider but faces ongoing challenges in its business services segment.
  
    > “Verizon continued to see business service opportunities and struggles during the second quarter.” [1](https://www.lightwaveonline.com/home/article/55313518/att-remains-the-dominant-ethernet-provider)
    
   - Confirmed victim of Salt Typhoon cyber espionage targeting call records among senior officials[4](https://www.sovereignmagazine.com/science-tech/artificial-intelligence/why-salt-typhoon-hack-changes-everything-about-national/) [5](https://techcrunch.com/2025/08/27/fbi-says-chinas-salt-typhoon-hacked-at-least-200-us-companies/)
   
#### **Lumen**

   - Focused on becoming a *business-centric* service provider after selling its consumer fiber assets to AT&T.

   - Expanding metro & long-haul fiber networks with an emphasis on supporting AI workloads through Private Connectivity Fabric solutions.

   - Despite strategic moves toward enterprise networking for AI applications:
     *Business segment revenue fell by 3.4% in Q2*, mainly due to declines in VPN^[Virtual Private Network] & Ethernet revenue.
