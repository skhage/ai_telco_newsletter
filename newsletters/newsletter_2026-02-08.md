In data tech expansions, AT&T introduced **AT&T IoT Network Intelligence** for enterprise device visibility, targeting sectors like healthcare, transportation, and logistics to reduce legacy service reliance.[1](https://finviz.com/news/277588/t-vs-chtr-which-telecom-stock-has-more-upside-right-now) Partnerships include Mitsubishi Motors for 5G in the Outlander SUV and **AST SpaceMobile** for satellite-to-cellular coverage in underserved areas, addressing terrestrial network gaps.[1](https://finviz.com/news/277588/t-vs-chtr-which-telecom-stock-has-more-upside-right-now) However, AT&T faces contractor woes, paying only 15% of invoices within 30 days and 45% within 60, exacerbating workforce attrition in wireless builds.[2](https://potsandpansbyccg.com/tag/t-mobile/) FCC fines related to lower court cases underscore ongoing compliance issues alongside T-Mobile and Verizon.[2](https://potsandpansbyccg.com/tag/t-mobile/)
