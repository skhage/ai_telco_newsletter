**Key Industry Trends in U.S. Data Technology (Week of November 12, 2025)**

- **Mergers and Acquisitions (M&A) are accelerating** as fiber demand and competition intensify, with major players acquiring regional fiber assets and fixed wireless providers to expand broadband reach and densify infrastructure[1](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/)[4](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/articles/2025/10/moorgate-sees-fiber-demand-driving-m-a-among-large-midsize-operators-into-2026-94055375).
- **Customer service is becoming a central competitive differentiator**. Leading broadband service providers (BSPs) are shifting focus from just speed and price to service guarantees, multi-year price locks, and same-day support, especially in the wake of major mergers[2](https://www.telecompetitor.com/bsps-made-big-customer-service-pledges-in-2025-but-can-they-deliver/).
- **Fixed Wireless Access (FWA) and 5G adoption are surging**, with operators leveraging new spectrum and urban network assets to reach more households and businesses, particularly in dense metro areas[1](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/)[3](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/vz-vs-chtr-which-connectivity-stock-better-buy-now).
- **Fiber-to-the-Home (FTTH)^[Fiber to the Home] expansion remains a top priority**, with operators racing to achieve critical mass and scale through both organic buildouts and acquisitions[4](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/articles/2025/10/moorgate-sees-fiber-demand-driving-m-a-among-large-midsize-operators-into-2026-94055375).

### AT&T

- **Acquisition of Lumen’s Mass Markets FTTH business**: AT&T is finalizing its purchase of Lumen Technologies’ consumer fiber assets, a move that will significantly expand AT&T’s FTTH footprint and densify its existing fiber infrastructure[2](https://www.telecompetitor.com/bsps-made-big-customer-service-pledges-in-2025-but-can-they-deliver/)[3](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/vz-vs-chtr-which-connectivity-stock-better-buy-now)[4](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/articles/2025/10/moorgate-sees-fiber-demand-driving-m-a-among-large-midsize-operators-into-2026-94055375).
- **Strategic focus**: AT&T is aggressively expanding its fiber network to compete with Verizon and Charter, aiming to leverage scale for operational efficiencies and improved customer experience[3](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/vz-vs-chtr-which-connectivity-stock-better-buy-now)[4](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/articles/2025/10/moorgate-sees-fiber-demand-driving-m-a-among-large-midsize-operators-into-2026-94055375).

### T-Mobile

- **Fiber Expansion via M&A**: T-Mobile, in partnership with KKR, acquired Metronet, a major FTTH provider, and recently completed the acquisition of U.S. Internet (USI), a Minneapolis-based fiber operator. USI’s residential customers will transition to T-Mobile Fiber, while commercial clients move under Metronet Commercial[1](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/).
- **Lumos Acquisition**: Earlier this year, T-Mobile purchased Lumos, further strengthening its fiber portfolio and accelerating its FTTH growth strategy[2](https://www.telecompetitor.com/bsps-made-big-customer-service-pledges-in-2025-but-can-they-deliver/).
- **Customer Service Initiatives**: T-Mobile is emphasizing improved customer experience and service guarantees as it integrates new acquisitions and expands its fiber and wireless offerings[2](https://www.telecompetitor.com/bsps-made-big-customer-service-pledges-in-2025-but-can-they-deliver/).
- **Text-to-911 Expansion**: T-Mobile announced broader availability of text-to-911 services, enhancing accessibility and public safety for its users[6](https://www.lightreading.com/latest-press-release).
