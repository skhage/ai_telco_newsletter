**Key Industry Trends (Week of September 17, 2025)**

- **Carrier Ethernet Market Stabilization:** The U.S. Carrier Ethernet^[Carrier Ethernet: a standardized, high-bandwidth, business-grade data service] market is maturing, with AT&T, Lumen, Verizon, and Charter (Spectrum Business) all within a 4% share of each other. Fiber investments and next-gen service rollouts are driving competition, while legacy TDM^[Time Division Multiplexing] service revenues continue to decline[1](https://www.lightwaveonline.com/home/article/55313518/att-remains-the-dominant-ethernet-provider).
- **Fiber Expansion and M&A:** AT&T’s acquisition of Lumen’s consumer fiber business and T-Mobile’s JV-backed Metronet acquisition of USI highlight aggressive moves to expand fiber footprints and consolidate market share[3](https://www.ainvest.com/news/reaffirms-fiscal-2025-guidance-confirms-capital-return-plans-2509/)[5](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/).
- **AI-Ready Networks and Enterprise Focus:** Lumen and Verizon are pivoting to enterprise services, with Lumen emphasizing AI-ready network architectures and Verizon strengthening managed services through strategic partnerships[1](https://www.lightwaveonline.com/home/article/55313518/att-remains-the-dominant-ethernet-provider).
- **Cable Operators Target Business Data:** Charter and Comcast are leveraging wireless partnerships (notably Charter with T-Mobile) and cloud offerings to challenge telcos in the business data market[1](https://www.lightwaveonline.com/home/article/55313518/att-remains-the-dominant-ethernet-provider).
- **Cybersecurity Threats:** Major U.S. telecoms, including all five companies, were targeted by a large-scale Chinese state-backed cyberattack, raising concerns about national data infrastructure security[4](https://www.sovereignmagazine.com/science-tech/artificial-intelligence/why-salt-typhoon-hack-changes-everything-about-national/).

### AT&T

- **Ethernet Leadership:** AT&T remains the dominant U.S. Carrier Ethernet provider, driven by continued investment in fiber and advanced connectivity services. The company is reinvesting cost savings to accelerate fiber and next-gen service growth[1](https://www.lightwaveonline.com/home/article/55313518/att-remains-the-dominant-ethernet-provider).
- **Fiber Expansion:** AT&T reaffirmed its 2025 guidance, reporting strong growth in its Consumer Wireline segment, with fiber broadband revenues up 5.8% year-over-year and 243,000 net fiber additions in the latest quarter. Its nationwide fiber footprint now reaches 30 million locations[3](https://www.ainvest.com/news/reaffirms-fiscal-2025-guidance-confirms-capital-return-plans-2509/).
- **M&A Activity:** The $5.75 billion acquisition of Lumen’s consumer fiber business will add 1 million fiber customers and 4 million locations across 11 states, further cementing AT&T’s fiber leadership[3](https://www.ainvest.com/news/reaffirms-fiscal-2025-guidance-confirms-capital-return-plans-2509/)[5](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/).
- **Security Incident:** AT&T was among the major carriers targeted by the Salt Typhoon cyberattack, highlighting ongoing vulnerabilities in national telecom infrastructure[4](https://www.sovereignmagazine.com/science-tech/artificial-intelligence/why-salt-typhoon-hack-changes-everything-about-national/).
