**Key Industry Trends (Week of August 20, 2025)**

- **Aggressive fiber and FTTH^[Fiber to the Home] expansion** is reshaping the U.S. data technology landscape, with major M&A activity and capital investments by AT&T, Verizon, Charter, and Lumen.
- **Cable–wireless convergence** is accelerating, as Charter and T-Mobile announce new partnerships targeting business wireless and MVNO^[Mobile Virtual Network Operator] markets, challenging Verizon’s traditional dominance.
- **Streaming bundles and broadband integration** are central to cable strategies, with Charter launching bold new streaming packages to combat cord-cutting and drive broadband growth.
- **Earnings resilience**: Despite macroeconomic headwinds, sector leaders (AT&T, Verizon) posted strong Q2 results, buoyed by wireless and fiber revenue, and investor confidence is high in telecom ETFs.

---

### AT&T

- **Q2 2025 earnings exceeded expectations**, driven by robust wireless service revenue and continued investment in 5G and fiber infrastructure[3](https://www.ainvest.com/news/momentum-driven-growth-communications-services-unpacking-high-conviction-opportunities-risk-environment-2508/).
- **Major M&A:** AT&T announced a $5.8 billion acquisition of Lumen Technologies, signaling a strategic push to expand FTTH^[Fiber to the Home] and future-proof its network for AI-driven and cloud-based demand[3](https://www.ainvest.com/news/momentum-driven-growth-communications-services-unpacking-high-conviction-opportunities-risk-environment-2508/).
- **Competitive positioning:** AT&T’s moves are part of a broader sector trend toward infrastructure consolidation and scaling to meet enterprise and consumer data needs.

### T-Mobile

- **Business MVNO breakthrough:** T-Mobile inked a new business-focused MVNO deal with Charter (and Comcast), positioning itself as a key wholesale provider for the nation’s top cable operators[1](https://www.lightwaveonline.com/home/article/55308736/charters-ceo-says-t-mobile-partnership-opens-the-door-for-business-wireless-sales)[2](https://www.lightreading.com/5g/t-mobile-s-new-cable-deal-winners-and-losers).
- **Strategic implications:** This deal gives T-Mobile a strong foothold in business wireless, potentially threatening Verizon’s dominance as existing MVNO agreements come up for renegotiation in 2026[2](https://www.lightreading.com/5g/t-mobile-s-new-cable-deal-winners-and-losers).
- **Incremental growth:** T-Mobile’s CEO described the new agreement as “incremental” but analysts see it as a credible threat in the ongoing cable–wireless convergence[2](https://www.lightreading.com/5g/t-mobile-s-new-cable-deal-winners-and-losers).

### Verizon

- **Q2 2025 outperformance:** Verizon delivered adjusted earnings of $1.22 per share, exceeding analyst expectations, with growth driven by wireless service revenue and capital investments in 5G and fiber[3](https://www.ainvest.com/news/momentum-driven-growth-communications-services-unpacking-high-conviction-opportunities-risk-environment-2508/).
- **Fiber expansion:** CEO Hans Vestberg reported the company is ahead of schedule to deliver 650,000 incremental fiber passings this year, underscoring Verizon’s commitment to network modernization[5](https://www.mobileworldlive.com/att/analysis-who-is-winning-t-mobile-att-verizons-fibre-brawl/).
- **MVNO headwinds:** The new T-Mobile–Charter–Comcast deal may complicate Verizon’s renegotiation of its own cable MVNO contracts, potentially eroding its wholesale market share[2](https://www.lightreading.com/5g/t-mobile-s-new-cable-deal-winners-and-losers).

### Lumen Technologies

- **Acquisition by AT&T:** Lumen is being acquired for $5.8 billion, a move that highlights the value of its FTTH^[Fiber to the Home] assets and positions AT&T to accelerate its fiber rollout[3](https://www.ainvest.com/news/momentum-driven-growth-communications-services-unpacking-high-conviction-opportunities-risk-environment-2508/).
- **Strategic context:** The deal is part of a sector-wide race to scale fiber infrastructure and meet surging demand for high-speed, low-latency connectivity.
