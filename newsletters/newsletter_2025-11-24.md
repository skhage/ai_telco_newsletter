**Key Industry Trends in U.S. Data Technology (Week of Nov 18–24, 2025)**

- **Aggressive Fiber Expansion:** AT&T^[American Telephone & Telegraph] and T-Mobile^[T-Mobile US] are driving major fiber acquisitions, while Verizon^[Verizon Communications] is doubling down on fixed wireless and urban fiber strategies. Charter^[Charter Communications] continues to grow its broadband footprint, with industry-wide focus on densifying fiber networks and hybrid approaches to reach underserved areas[1](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/vz-vs-chtr-which-connectivity-stock-better-buy-now)[2](https://www.fierce-network.com/broadband/fierce-networks-2025-telecom-and-tech-ma-tracker)[3](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/)[4](https://sundaybrief.com/verizons-dilemma/).
- **M&A Activity:** The week saw continued consolidation, with AT&T closing its acquisition of Lumen’s consumer fiber business and T-Mobile acquiring regional ISP U.S. Internet. Verizon’s pending acquisition of Starry, Inc. signals further urban broadband expansion[2](https://www.fierce-network.com/broadband/fierce-networks-2025-telecom-and-tech-ma-tracker)[3](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/).
- **Cybersecurity Focus:** Following last year’s Salt Typhoon espionage campaign, all major telcos are coordinating on cybersecurity improvements, though the FCC has rolled back some federal requirements, placing more responsibility on providers[5](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/fcc-rolls-back-cybersecurity-rules-for-telcos-despite-state-hacking-risks/).
- **Fixed Wireless Growth:** Fixed wireless access (FWA) continues to drive net broadband additions, especially for Verizon and T-Mobile, as fiber buildouts remain capital-intensive[6](https://broadbandbreakfast.com/chart-of-fixed-wireless-access-subscribers/).

---

### AT&T

- **Lumen Fiber Acquisition:** AT&T finalized its $5.75B purchase of Lumen’s Mass Markets FTTH^[Fiber to the Home] business, adding nearly 1 million subscribers and 4 million passings. This move significantly expands AT&T’s fiber footprint and positions it as a leading U.S. fiber provider[2](https://www.fierce-network.com/broadband/fierce-networks-2025-telecom-and-tech-ma-tracker)[1](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/vz-vs-chtr-which-connectivity-stock-better-buy-now).
- **Fiber Densification:** AT&T is focused on densifying its existing fiber infrastructure, aiming to challenge Verizon’s network expansion and capture more market share in high-speed broadband[1](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/vz-vs-chtr-which-connectivity-stock-better-buy-now).
- **Cybersecurity Coordination:** AT&T, impacted by the Salt Typhoon attacks, is participating in industry-wide efforts to strengthen cybersecurity, following the FCC’s regulatory rollback[5](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/fcc-rolls-back-cybersecurity-rules-for-telcos-despite-state-hacking-risks/).

---

### T-Mobile

- **U.S. Internet Acquisition:** T-Mobile acquired Minnesota-based ISP U.S. Internet, furthering its fiber ambitions. The deal complements earlier acquisitions of Lumos and Metronet, with T-Mobile targeting 12–15 million homes passed with fiber by 2030[2](https://www.fierce-network.com/broadband/fierce-networks-2025-telecom-and-tech-ma-tracker)[3](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/).
- **Fiber Expansion Strategy:** T-Mobile’s joint venture with EQT for Lumos is now closed, accelerating its FTTH rollout in new markets[2](https://www.fierce-network.com/broadband/fierce-networks-2025-telecom-and-tech-ma-tracker).
- **Cybersecurity Response:** T-Mobile is among the providers enhancing cybersecurity post-Salt Typhoon, in coordination with peers and regulators[5](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/fcc-rolls-back-cybersecurity-rules-for-telcos-despite-state-hacking-risks/).
