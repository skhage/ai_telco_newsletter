**Key Industry Trends (Week of September 29, 2025)**

- **Carrier Ethernet and Fiber Expansion:** The U.S. Carrier Ethernet^[Carrier-grade Ethernet, a standardized, high-performance Ethernet for telecom networks] market is stabilizing, with AT&T, Lumen, Verizon, and Charter (Spectrum Business) all within a 4% share difference, reflecting a mature and highly competitive landscape. Fiber investments and next-gen service rollouts are central to growth strategies, especially for AT&T and T-Mobile^[T-Mobile US, a major wireless carrier] via its fiber JV^[Joint Venture] with KKR[1](https://www.lightwaveonline.com/home/article/55313518/att-remains-the-dominant-ethernet-provider)[3](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/).

- **Strategic M&A and Asset Realignment:** Major M&A activity continues, notably AT&T’s acquisition of Lumen’s consumer fiber assets and T-Mobile’s expansion through Metronet’s acquisition of U.S. Internet. These moves are reshaping competitive dynamics and accelerating FTTH^[Fiber to the Home] deployment[3](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/).

- **Mobile/Wireline Convergence:** Analysts expect 2026 to be pivotal as mobile and wireline services converge, intensifying competition between telcos and cable operators. Wireless partnerships and bundled offerings are becoming key differentiators[4](https://www.ainvest.com/news/goldman-sachs-predicts-2026-pivotal-year-telecom-cable-sectors-2509/).

---

### AT&T

- **Ethernet Leadership:** AT&T remains the dominant U.S. Carrier Ethernet provider, driven by ongoing fiber investments and next-generation service deployments. The company is reinvesting cost savings into expanding its fiber and advanced connectivity portfolio[1](https://www.lightwaveonline.com/home/article/55313518/att-remains-the-dominant-ethernet-provider).

- **Business Wireline Pressure:** AT&T’s Business Wireline segment continues to face secular revenue declines from legacy TDM^[Time Division Multiplexing, a legacy telecom technology]-based services, partially offset by growth in fiber and advanced connectivity. Q2 operating income fell by $201 million year-over-year[1](https://www.lightwaveonline.com/home/article/55313518/att-remains-the-dominant-ethernet-provider).

- **M&A Activity:** AT&T completed the $5.74 billion acquisition of Lumen’s consumer fiber business in May, strengthening its FTTH footprint and allowing Lumen to focus on enterprise services[3](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/).

---

### T-Mobile

- **Fiber Expansion via Metronet JV:** T-Mobile, through its joint venture with KKR, is aggressively expanding its fiber reach. The recent acquisition of U.S. Internet (USI), a Minneapolis-based fiber provider, adds over 140,000 homes and businesses to its footprint. USI’s residential customers will transition to T-Mobile Fiber, while commercial clients move under Metronet Commercial[3](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/).

- **Wireless Partnerships:** T-Mobile is deepening its relationship with Charter, aiming to bundle mobile and wireline services for business customers, a move expected to drive growth in the SMB and enterprise segments[1](https://www.lightwaveonline.com/home/article/55313518/att-remains-the-dominant-ethernet-provider).

---

### Verizon

- **Managed Services Focus:** Verizon is strengthening its managed services portfolio, notably through partnerships such as with HCLTech, aiming to improve EBITDA^[Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization] margins and expand service offerings for enterprise clients[1](https://www.lightwaveonline.com/home/article/55313518/att-remains-the-dominant-ethernet-provider).

- **Competitive Positioning:** Despite revenue pressures in VPN^[Virtual Private Network] and Ethernet, Verizon remains a top-tier player in the maturing Carrier Ethernet market, focusing on enterprise and managed solutions[1](https://www.lightwaveonline.com/home/article/55313518/att-remains-the-dominant-ethernet-provider).
