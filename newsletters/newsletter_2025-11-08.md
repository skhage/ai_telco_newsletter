**Key Industry Trends in U.S. Data Technology (Week of November 8, 2025)**

- **Major Mergers and Acquisitions:** The U.S. telecom landscape is being reshaped by a wave of mergers, with T-Mobile acquiring Lumos and U.S. Internet, Charter purchasing Cox, AT&T acquiring Lumen, and Verizon buying fixed wireless provider Starry. These moves are accelerating the convergence of fiber and wireless networks, intensifying competition and driving rapid infrastructure expansion[1](https://www.telecompetitor.com/bsps-made-big-customer-service-pledges-in-2025-but-can-they-deliver/)[4](https://wirelessestimator.com).
- **Customer Service as a Differentiator:** Amidst increased competition and merger activity, broadband service providers (BSPs) are shifting focus from just speed and price to **customer service guarantees**—including same-day service, contract freedom, and multi-year price guarantees[1](https://www.telecompetitor.com/bsps-made-big-customer-service-pledges-in-2025-but-can-they-deliver/).
- **Fiber and 5G Expansion:** All major players are aggressively expanding their **fiber** and **5G** footprints, with Charter investing $7 billion in fiber, AT&T densifying its fiber network, and Verizon pushing its 5G Ultra Wideband and fixed wireless access (FWA) offerings[2](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/vz-vs-chtr-which-connectivity-stock-better-buy-now).
- **Market Headwinds:** The end of the ACP^[Affordable Connectivity Program] and the rise of FWA^[Fixed Wireless Access] are causing subscriber losses for cable broadband, with fiber and mobile-only households accelerating the shift away from traditional cable[3](https://www.benton.org/headlines/new-street-research-end-acp-continued-drag-cable-broadband-market).

---

### AT&T

- **Acquisition of Lumen:** AT&T is finalizing its acquisition of Lumen’s fiber business, a move aimed at densifying its existing fiber infrastructure and expanding its footprint in both urban and underserved areas[1](https://www.telecompetitor.com/bsps-made-big-customer-service-pledges-in-2025-but-can-they-deliver/)[2](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/vz-vs-chtr-which-connectivity-stock-better-buy-now).
- **Fiber Expansion:** The company is aggressively rolling out new fiber routes, positioning itself as a direct competitor to both cable and wireless broadband providers.
- **Customer Service Initiatives:** Alongside its rivals, AT&T has introduced multi-year price guarantees and improved service commitments to retain and attract customers in a highly competitive market[1](https://www.telecompetitor.com/bsps-made-big-customer-service-pledges-in-2025-but-can-they-deliver/).

---

### T-Mobile

- **Acquisition Spree:** T-Mobile has acquired fiber internet provider Lumos and U.S. Internet in Minnesota, and, in partnership with KKR, purchased Metronet. These deals signal T-Mobile’s intent to become a major player in the fiber broadband market[1](https://www.telecompetitor.com/bsps-made-big-customer-service-pledges-in-2025-but-can-they-deliver/).
- **UScellular Integration:** T-Mobile completed its purchase of UScellular, promising improved network experiences and customer benefits as part of its “Un-carrier” strategy[1](https://www.telecompetitor.com/bsps-made-big-customer-service-pledges-in-2025-but-can-they-deliver/).
- **Service Guarantees:** T-Mobile is emphasizing customer experience, offering value-packed benefits and best-in-class service as part of its integration efforts.

### Verizon

- **Starry Acquisition:** Verizon announced its acquisition of Starry, a fixed wireless broadband provider specializing in urban multi-dwelling units, with the deal expected to close in early 2026[1](https://www.telecompetitor.com/bsps-made-big-customer-service-pledges-in-2025-but-can-they-deliver/)[4](https://wirelessestimator.com).
- **Leadership Change:** Dan Schulman, former PayPal chief, has been named CEO, succeeding Hans Vestberg, who will remain as a special adviser through 2026 to assist with the integration of Frontier[4](https://wirelessestimator.com).
