### Key Data Technology Trends in US Telecom (Week of Jan 5-7, 2026)

The past week highlighted ongoing **fiber asset consolidation** among major carriers, with AT&T advancing its acquisition of **Lumen**'s mass markets fiber business for $5.75B to bolster broadband/mobile convergence, while **T-Mobile**'s joint venture with KKR (via Metronet) integrated new fiber acquisitions for rapid **FTTH^[Fiber to the Home]** expansion.[1](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/)[2](https://onetouchintelligence.com/insights/)[3](https://www.telecompetitor.com/broadband-ma-2025-dominated-by-private-equity-buys-big-guys-made-strategic-deals/) **Fixed wireless access (FWA)** growth persists amid spectrum maneuvers, as carriers like **Verizon** eye urban mmWave^[millimeter-wave] integrations and **T-Mobile** implements data prioritization caps on its 5M FWA subscribers.[1](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/)[2](https://onetouchintelligence.com/insights/) Broader pressures include tariff impacts on broadband costs, permitting reform debates tied to **BEAD^[Broadband Equity, Access, and Deployment]** rollout, and legal disputes over toll-free call tariffs, signaling rising operational frictions in data infrastructure.[2](https://onetouchintelligence.com/insights/)[5](https://www.law360.com/telecom)

### AT&T
AT&T continues aggressive fiber expansion through its pending $5.75B acquisition of **Lumen**'s mass markets fiber assets, a deal announced earlier but progressing amid 2026 M&A momentum to enable broader **broadband/mobile convergence**—where bundled services reduce churn and boost revenue.[1](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/)[2](https://onetouchintelligence.com/insights/)[3](https://www.telecompetitor.com/broadband-ma-2025-dominated-by-private-equity-buys-big-guys-made-strategic-deals/) This complements AT&T's rapid deployment of **Gigapower** fiber, with CEO John Stankey noting early customer sign-ups pre-Q1 2025 earnings, fueling Q4 2024 fiber gains for convergence opportunities.[2](https://onetouchintelligence.com/insights/) Additionally, AT&T is deploying **EchoStar**'s 3.45 GHz mid-band spectrum ahead of its $23B acquisition close, enhancing **5G** capacity for data-heavy FWA like **Internet Air**, now expanding to 16 new markets to rival T-Mobile and Verizon.[2](https://onetouchintelligence.com/insights/)

### T-Mobile
**T-Mobile**'s fiber strategy advanced via its KKR-Metronet JV, which acquired **U.S. Internet (USI)**—a Minneapolis fiber provider serving 140,000+ homes/businesses with up to 10 Gbps symmetrical speeds—accelerating **FTTH** to 2.6M+ locations across 19 states; USI residential users shift to **T-Mobile Fiber** for sales/marketing.[1](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/)[3](https://www.telecompetitor.com/broadband-ma-2025-dominated-by-private-equity-buys-big-guys-made-strategic-deals/) T-Mobile also inked a spectrum lease with **Comcast** for 71 licenses in the 600 MHz band (covering 149M POPs), supporting FWA leadership despite introducing a soft data cap on its 5M FWA subscribers to manage prioritization.[2](https://onetouchintelligence.com/insights/) Talks continue for anchor tenancy on **Tillman FiberCo**'s open-access network, while Q4 fiber gains position T-Mobile for convergence plays akin to its **Lumos** JV with EQT.[2](https://onetouchintelligence.com/insights/)[3](https://www.telecompetitor.com/broadband-ma-2025-dominated-by-private-equity-buys-big-guys-made-strategic-deals/)
