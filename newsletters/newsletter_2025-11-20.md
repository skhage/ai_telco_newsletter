**Key Industry Trends in U.S. Data Technology (Week of November 20, 2025)**

- **Aggressive Fiber Expansion:** Major telcos including AT&T^[American Telephone & Telegraph], T-Mobile^[T-Mobile US], Verizon^[Verizon Communications], and Charter^[Charter Communications] are accelerating fiber deployments, with M&A activity reshaping the competitive landscape[4](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/articles/2025/10/moorgate-sees-fiber-demand-driving-m-a-among-large-midsize-operators-into-2026-94055375).
- **Fixed Wireless Growth:** Fixed wireless access (FWA) continues to gain traction, driven by Verizon, T-Mobile, and AT&T, now serving over 13 million subscribers—up from near zero four years ago[8](https://broadbandbreakfast.com/chart-of-fixed-wireless-access-subscribers/).
- **Consolidation & M&A:** The pace of mergers and acquisitions remains high, with AT&T acquiring Lumen’s fiber assets, Charter buying Cox, and T-Mobile expanding its fiber footprint through multiple deals[2](https://www.fierce-network.com/broadband/fierce-networks-2025-telecom-and-tech-ma-tracker)[3](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/).
- **5G and Edge Compute:** Operators are investing in 5G Ultra Wideband and edge computing to support next-gen connectivity and enterprise solutions[1](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/vz-vs-chtr-which-connectivity-stock-better-buy-now).

### AT&T

- **Fiber Expansion:** AT&T hit **30 million fiber locations** ahead of schedule, intensifying competition in the broadband market[5](https://www.zacks.com/stock/research/T/all-news). The company is aggressively expanding its FTTH^[Fiber to the Home] footprint and densifying its existing fiber infrastructure[1](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/vz-vs-chtr-which-connectivity-stock-better-buy-now).
- **Acquisition of Lumen’s Fiber Assets:** AT&T completed its $5.75 billion acquisition of Lumen’s consumer fiber business, adding nearly 1 million subscribers and 4 million passings. This move strengthens AT&T’s position in the fiber race and challenges rivals’ expansion plans[2](https://www.fierce-network.com/broadband/fierce-networks-2025-telecom-and-tech-ma-tracker)[4](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/articles/2025/10/moorgate-sees-fiber-demand-driving-m-a-among-large-midsize-operators-into-2026-94055375).

### T-Mobile

- **Fiber M&A Surge:** T-Mobile, through its joint ventures, acquired Minnesota-based ISP U.S. Internet, further expanding its fiber broadband footprint. The integration will see residential customers transition to T-Mobile Fiber, with commercial clients moving under Metronet Commercial[2](https://www.fierce-network.com/broadband/fierce-networks-2025-telecom-and-tech-ma-tracker)[3](https://tecknexus.com/telecom-and-tech-merger-and-acquisition-tracker-2025/).
- **Lumos and Metronet Deals:** T-Mobile’s JV with EQT to acquire Lumos and its ownership of Metronet position it to pass 12–15 million homes with fiber by 2030, making it a major FTTH contender[2](https://www.fierce-network.com/broadband/fierce-networks-2025-telecom-and-tech-ma-tracker)[4](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/articles/2025/10/moorgate-sees-fiber-demand-driving-m-a-among-large-midsize-operators-into-2026-94055375).
- **Fixed Wireless Leadership:** T-Mobile remains a leader in FWA, contributing significantly to the industry’s 13 million+ fixed wireless subscribers[4](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/articles/2025/10/moorgate-sees-fiber-demand-driving-m-a-among-large-midsize-operators-into-2026-94055375)[8](https://broadbandbreakfast.com/chart-of-fixed-wireless-access-subscribers/).
