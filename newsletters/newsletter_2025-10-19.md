## AT&T

- **Fiber Acquisition:** AT&T agreed to buy Lumen’s mass-market fiber assets for $5.75B, boosting its FTTH^[Fiber to the Home] footprint and competitive position in key U.S. markets[4](https://onetouchintelligence.com/insights/).
- **Spectrum Expansion:** AT&T will acquire ~50 MHz of low- and mid-band spectrum licenses from EchoStar for $23B, covering 400+ markets and narrowing its mid-band gap with competitors. This move is expected to improve network capacity for consumer, enterprise, and public-sector growth over the next 5–7 years[1](https://tecknexus.com/keyword/att/).
- **AI Call-Screening:** AT&T is testing a carrier-grade AI “digital receptionist” that screens unknown calls, filters robocalls, and leverages calling history for smarter routing[1](https://tecknexus.com/keyword/att/).
- **Subway 5G Rollout:** AT&T went live on Boldyn Networks’ neutral-host infrastructure in NYC’s Joralemon Street tunnel, providing 5G service in the oldest underwater subway tunnel. Other carriers are expected to follow as onboarding progresses[1](https://tecknexus.com/keyword/att/).
- **FWA Expansion:** AT&T expanded its Internet Air FWA service to 16 new markets, signaling intent to compete more aggressively with T-Mobile and Verizon in wireless broadband[4](https://onetouchintelligence.com/insights/).

## T-Mobile

- **LA28 Olympics Partnership:** T-Mobile for Business will be the Official Telecommunications Services Provider for the LA28 Olympic and Paralympic Games, supporting 5G operations, broadcast connectivity, and fan experience across 110+ locations. This event will test wide-area 5G, edge compute, and venue networks under peak loads[1](https://tecknexus.com/keyword/att/).
- **MVNO Cable Deal:** T-Mobile, Charter, and Comcast are the main winners in a new business-focused MVNO agreement, which could impact Verizon’s residential MVNO arrangement with cable operators[3](https://www.lightreading.com/network-technology/broadband/fixed-wireless-access).
- **Fixed Wireless Data Cap:** T-Mobile instituted a soft data cap for its 5M FWA subscribers, introducing speed slowdowns for heavy users as part of its data prioritization strategy[4](https://onetouchintelligence.com/insights/).
- **Open-Access Fiber Talks:** T-Mobile is reportedly in discussions with Tillman FiberCo to become an anchor tenant on a new open-access fiber network, potentially expanding its fiber reach[4](https://onetouchintelligence.com/insights/).
- **Spectrum Lease:** T-Mobile and Comcast agreed to a deal for T-Mobile to lease 71 spectrum licenses in the 600 MHz band, covering 149M people[4](https://onetouchintelligence.com/insights/).
